Incline DB Chest Press
Chest · Sunday
▼
Setup

Seat fully upright. This is your ego lift — 40–45 lb, load it and commit. Incline targets upper chest.

Execution

Drive hard. Full press. Primary heavy chest movement, comes first when you're fresh.

Technique tip

Retract shoulder blades into the pad before pressing. Keeps the shoulder joint stable and takes the front delt out of it.

Incline DB Fly
Chest · Tuesday
▼
Setup

Seat up, 45° incline. Weights meeting in the middle — like hugging a barrel. Slight bend in elbows, locked throughout.

Key cue

The bottom of the movement matters more than the top. Control the stretch on the way down. The eccentric is where the growth stimulus is.

Technique tip

Think hug, not press. If you feel it in your shoulders, your elbows are too straight — maintain that slight bend.

Dips
Chest · Thursday
▼
Setup

Lean forward to bias the chest. The more upright you stay, the more it becomes a tricep movement.

Range

Chest drops below hands at the bottom. Full range, controlled descent.

Technique tip

If bodyweight gets easy, add a dumbbell between your feet. Keep it close to failure — don't let it become an endurance exercise.

Push-ups to Failure
Chest Finisher · Thursday
▼
Purpose

Closes Thursday after chest is already taxed from dips. Burnout — absolute failure. Don't pace it.

Technique

Hands slightly wider than shoulder width, elbows at ~45°. Chest touches the floor each rep. When full reps are gone, finish with negatives.

Lat Pulldown (Drop Set)
Back · Sunday
▼
Phase 1 — Wide grip

Elbows in. Pull to failure — stop when form breaks.

Phase 2 — Closer grip

Move hands in immediately. Pull down past nipple height to failure.

Phase 3 — Single arm

Swap to one hand, pull in to failure. No rest between phases.

Technique tip

Drive elbows down toward your hips, not pulling with your hands. Keeps lats engaged instead of biceps taking over.

Seated Cable Rows
Back · Tuesday
▼
Setup

Feet braced against foot plate. Slide shoulders down the pad before each rep.

Key cue

Pull around nipple height. Control the stretch on the eccentric — don't let it snap back.

Technique tip

At peak contraction, hold and squeeze shoulder blades together. Most people skip this and it's where half the back engagement lives.

Chest-Supported Row
Back · Thursday
▼
Setup

Legs against foot plate, chest on the pad. Removes lower back entirely — can't cheat with momentum.

Key cue

The forward movement (eccentric) is the main event. Pull hard, release slowly.

DB Shoulder Press
Shoulder · Sunday
▼
Setup

Seat at max height. Lighter weight than you think — full range of motion is the priority.

Range

All the way down until dumbbells reach shoulder level. At the top, push arms back to be inline with your torso — full lockout, arms slightly behind the body plane.

Technique tip

Don't let your lower back arch away from the pad as you press. If it does, the weight is too heavy.

DB Lateral Raise
Shoulder · Tuesday
▼
Setup

Pelvic tilt to brace the core. Lean slightly forward. Light weight — burn movement, not load movement.

Path

Sweep out at ~45° from your body — making a Y shape, not a T. Lead with elbows, not hands.

Technique tip

Stop just below shoulder height — going higher recruits the traps and the lateral delt disengages.

Rear Delt Raise
Shoulder · Thursday
▼
Setup

Chest face-down on the 45° incline bench. Lighter than the lateral raise.

Path

Sweep out at 45° — making a Y. No momentum. Pure rear delt isolation.

Technique tip

If you feel it in your traps instead of the back of the shoulder, drop the weight and slow down. Precision over load.

Incline DB Curl
Bicep · All Days
▼
Grip rotation

Hammer (neutral) on the way up, supinate to horizontal on the way down.

Setup

Puff your chest up — keeps the shoulder from taking over.

Range

Arms all the way down on every rep. The full stretch at the bottom is the entire reason you're on an incline bench.

Technique tip

Upper arm stays perpendicular to the floor — don't let it swing forward. If it drifts, you've lost the stretch advantage.

Rope Tricep Pushdown
Tricep · Sunday & Thursday
▼
Setup

Ass out, bent forward ~45°. Not stand-straight-and-push — the hinge keeps the long head under tension.

Key cue

Pin the elbows — they are the hinge point, nothing else moves. If elbows drift forward, drop the weight.

Technique tip

At the bottom, spread the rope ends slightly apart and squeeze. That flare gives a stronger contraction than pushing straight down.

Decline DB Skull Crushers
Tricep · Tuesday
▼
Grip

Lightly rotated inward — not fully neutral, not fully pronated.

Path

Lower to ears / side of head. Decline increases the stretch on the long head — that's the point.

Key cue

Control the eccentric hard. Upper arms stay stationary, elbows are the hinge only.

Technique tip

If your wrists are taking strain, rotate inward slightly more until the wrist is neutral through the full range.
